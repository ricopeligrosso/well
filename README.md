# THE GREEN HAT MANIFESTO

## What This Is

A declaration for a global transition to a well-being society.

A well-being society guarantees human dignity while staying within planetary limits.  
Economic activity exists to serve people and the planetâ€”not the other way around.

Green Hat Hackers are a decentralized, anonymous collective.  
No leaders. No owners. No profit motive.

---

## Core Principle

Human survival, social stability, and ecological integrity are non-negotiable.

Any system that undermines these must be corrected.

---

## I. DEMOCRACY AND POWER

### Outcome
Democracy protected from economic capture.

### Objectives
- Politics and economic power must be strictly separated.
- Public decisions must be made in public interest.

### Commitments
- Lobbyism is abolished.
- Political funding is public, transparent, and capped.
- Revolving doors between government and corporations are banned.
- Religious institutions are taxed unless funds are directly used for social support.
- Gender, identity, belief, or origin shall have no influence on rights or treatment.

### Impact
- Restored trust in democratic systems.
- Decisions aligned with collective well-being.

---

## II. ECONOMY AND WEALTH

### Outcome
An economy that circulates value instead of concentrating it.

### Objectives
- Prevent extreme wealth accumulation.
- Stop intergenerational dominance.
- End tax avoidance.

### Commitments
- Inheritance is heavily taxed to prevent dynastic wealth.
- Extreme wealth is defined relative to the median, not fixed numbers.
- Capital accumulation across generations is capped.
- Profits are taxed where consumption happens.
- Tax havens are eliminated.
- Tax systems are simplified for regular citizens.
- Philanthropy does not replace systemic justice.

### Impact
- Fair starting conditions.
- Reduced inequality.
- Social stability.

---

## III. HOUSING AND LAND

### Outcome
Housing as a guaranteed right.

### Objectives
- End speculation.
- Ensure long-term housing security.

### Commitments
- Private speculative investment in housing is prohibited.
- An independent Central Housing Authority builds and governs housing.
- Housing is accessed through long-term leases, not ownership.
- Families retain long-term usage rights.
- Short-term rentals are regulated:
  - Every square meter of short-term use requires one square meter of new long-term housing.

### Impact
- Stable rents.
- Livable cities.
- End of housing bubbles.

---

## IV. ECOLOGY AND LIMITS

### Outcome
Human activity within planetary boundaries.

### Objectives
- Rapid emissions reduction.
- Regenerative systems.

### Commitments
- Private jets are banned or taxed to fully internalize damage.
- Flights are taxed to make sustainable transport competitive.
- Food production transitions to organic and regenerative methods.
- Harmful pesticides are banned.
- Single-use plastics are heavily taxed.
- Sugar is taxed due to public health impact.
- Climate limits are enforced as ceilings, not goals.

### Impact
- Emissions decline.
- Biodiversity recovery.
- Improved public health.

---

## V. SOCIAL RIGHTS

### Outcome
Universal access to dignity.

### Guaranteed Rights
- Food
- Housing
- Healthcare (public, universal)
- Education (free, lifelong)
- Internet access

### Additional Commitments
- Mental health is treated as equal to physical health.
- Care work is recognized and supported.

### Impact
- Reduced poverty.
- Higher resilience.
- Stronger communities.

---

## VI. EDUCATION AND SERVICE

### Outcome
An engaged and responsible population.

### Objectives
- Strengthen social cohesion.
- Replace militarism with service.

### Commitments
- Mandatory 12-month national service for all citizens from age 18.
- Service options include:
  - Ecological restoration
  - Care work
  - Education
  - Disaster response
  - Social services
- No distinction between students and non-students in public support.
- Education is lifelong.

### Impact
- Shared responsibility.
- Reduced social fragmentation.
- Strong public institutions.

---

## VII. DIGITAL COMMONS

### Outcome
Technology aligned with human well-being.

### Objectives
- Protect digital rights.
- Prevent technological domination.

### Commitments
- Personal data belongs to individuals.
- AI systems affecting society must be transparent and accountable.
- Digital infrastructure is treated as public infrastructure.
- Open standards and open knowledge are prioritized.

### Impact
- Digital sovereignty.
- Reduced exploitation.
- Safer technological progress.

---

## HOW WE ACT

Green Hat Hackers:
- Act ethically.
- Do not cause physical harm.
- Support democratic processes.
- Build, expose, educate, and reorganize.
- Create alternatives where systems fail.

Action starts with the individual and scales collectively.

---

## FINAL STATEMENT

This is not extremism.  
This is correction.

As long as these conditions are not met,  
Green Hat Hackers will use all non-violent means available  
to expose systemic harm  
and act against structures and actors  
that profit from destruction, exclusion, and inequality.

This is not a threat.  
It is a responsibility.
